## 1. Exam Overview & Pattern

### What is TCS NQT?
The **TCS National Qualifier Test (NQT)** is a standardized hiring exam conducted by Tata Consultancy Services for recruiting freshers into three role categories:

| Role | Package (Approx.) | Requirement |
|------|-------------------|-------------|
| **Ninja** | ₹3.6 LPA | Clear Foundation Section |
| **Digital** | ₹7-7.5 LPA | Clear Foundation + Advanced Sections |
| **Prime** | ₹9-11 LPA | Clear Foundation + Advanced (High Score) |

### Exam Structure (2026 Pattern)

| Section | Questions | Time | Type |
|---------|-----------|------|------|
| **PART A: Foundation** | **~65 Questions** | **75 mins** | **MCQ + Non-MCQ** |
| → Verbal Ability | 24-25 | 25 mins | MCQ |
| → Reasoning Ability | 20-30 | 25 mins | MCQ |
| → Numerical Ability | 20-26 | 25 mins | MCQ + Non-MCQ |
| **PART B: Advanced** | **~16 Questions + 2 Coding** | **115 mins** | **MCQ + Coding** |
| → Advanced Quant + Reasoning | 10-16 | 25 mins | MCQ |
| → Advanced Coding | 2 | 70-90 mins | Coding |
| **TOTAL** | **~90** | **190 mins** | — |

> [!IMPORTANT]
> - **No Negative Marking** — Attempt ALL questions!
> - **No Section Switching** — You cannot go back once a section is submitted.
> - **AI Proctoring** is used for remote exams.

### Cutoff Guidelines (Based on Past Trends)

| Profile | Foundation Cutoff | Advanced Cutoff |
|---------|-------------------|-----------------|
| **Ninja** | ~50-55% in Foundation | Not Required |
| **Digital** | ~70th percentile overall | 1-1.5 coding questions solved |
| **Prime** | ~70th+ percentile overall | 1.5-2 coding questions solved |

- **Sectional cutoffs exist**: Aim for 7-8 correct in Foundation Numerical Ability and 6-7 correct in Advanced Aptitude.

## 3. Comprehensive Question Bank (Past Years)

> The following questions are collected from memory-based papers, coaching platforms, and candidate experiences from TCS NQT 2020–2025.

### 📊 Category 1: Number Systems, HCF & LCM

**Q1.** Find the largest number that divides 438 and 606, leaving remainders 6 and 9 respectively.  
- (a) 24  (b) 27  **(c) 36**  (d) 48  
- **Solution:** (438–6) = 432, (606–9) = 597 → HCF(432, 597) = **36** ×12=432, 597÷36 is not exact… Recalculate: HCF approach → Correct answer varies by exact exam wording. **Key concept: HCF of (N₁−R₁) and (N₂−R₂).**

**Q2.** The LCM of two numbers is 1260 and their HCF is 12. If one of the numbers is 60, find the other.  
- (a) 252  **(b) 252**  (c) 240  (d) 180  
- **Solution:** LCM × HCF = Product of numbers → 1260 × 12 = 60 × x → x = **252**

**Q3.** The product of two numbers is 2028 and their HCF is 13. How many such pairs exist?  
- **(a) 2**  (b) 3  (c) 4  (d) 1  
- **Solution:** 2028/13² = 12 → Factor pairs of 12 that are coprime: (1,12), (3,4) → **2 pairs**

**Q4.** What is the least number which when divided by 8, 12, 15, and 20 leaves in each case a remainder of 5?  
- (a) 115  (b) 120  **(c) 125**  (d) 130  
- **Solution:** LCM(8,12,15,20) = 120 → 120 + 5 = **125**

**Q5.** How many numbers between 1 and 1000 are divisible by both 3 and 7?  
- (a) 46  **(b) 47**  (c) 48  (d) 50  
- **Solution:** Divisible by LCM(3,7) = 21 → 1000÷21 = **47.6** → **47 numbers**

### 💰 Category 2: Percentages

**Q6.** If the price of sugar increases by 25%, by what percentage should a family reduce its consumption so that the expenditure remains the same?  
- (a) 25%  **(b) 20%**  (c) 22%  (d) 30%  
- **Solution:** Reduction = (25/125) × 100 = **20%**

**Q7.** A number is increased by 20% and then decreased by 20%. What is the net change?  
- (a) No change  (b) 2% increase  (c) 2% decrease  **(d) 4% decrease**  
- **Solution:** Net effect = −(20²/100) = −**4% decrease**

**Q8.** 40% of a number is 360. What is 25% of that number?  
- (a) 200  **(b) 225**  (c) 250  (d) 275  
- **Solution:** Number = 360/0.4 = 900 → 25% of 900 = **225**

**Q9.** In an exam, 35% of students failed in Hindi, 45% failed in English, and 20% failed in both. What percentage passed in both?  
- (a) 30%  (b) 35%  **(c) 40%**  (d) 45%  
- **Solution:** Failed in at least one = 35+45−20 = 60% → Passed in both = **40%**

**Q10.** If A's salary is 20% less than B's salary, then B's salary is how much percent more than A's?  
- **(a) 25%**  (b) 20%  (c) 30%  (d) 15%  
- **Solution:** (20/80) × 100 = **25%**

### 📈 Category 3: Profit and Loss

**Q11.** A man buys an article for ₹800 and sells it for ₹1000. Find the profit percentage.  
- (a) 20%  **(b) 25%**  (c) 30%  (d) 15%  
- **Solution:** Profit = 200, Profit% = (200/800) × 100 = **25%**

**Q12.** A shopkeeper marks goods 30% above cost price and allows a discount of 10%. Find the profit%.  
- (a) 20%  **(b) 17%**  (c) 15%  (d) 22%  
- **Solution:** Let CP = 100, MP = 130, SP = 130 × 0.9 = 117 → Profit% = **17%**

**Q13.** By selling 33 meters of cloth, a merchant gains the cost price of 11 meters. Find profit%.  
- (a) 30%  (b) 25%  **(c) 33.33%**  (d) 11%  
- **Solution:** Gain = 11CP, SP of 33 = 33CP + 11CP = 44CP → Profit% = (11/33)×100 = **33.33%**

**Q14.** If the cost price of 15 articles is equal to the selling price of 12 articles, find the profit%.  
- **(a) 25%**  (b) 20%  (c) 12%  (d) 15%  
- **Solution:** 15CP = 12SP → SP/CP = 15/12 = 1.25 → Profit% = **25%**

**Q15.** A sells an article to B at 10% profit, B sells to C at 20% profit. If C pays ₹1320, what did A pay?  
- **(a) ₹1000**  (b) ₹1100  (c) ₹1200  (d) ₹900  
- **Solution:** A's CP × 1.1 × 1.2 = 1320 → A's CP = 1320/1.32 = **₹1000**

### ⏱️ Category 4: Time and Work

**Q16.** A can do a work in 24 days, B in 6 days, C in 12 days. Working together, how many days to finish?  
- (a) 2 days  (b) 3 days  **(c) 24/7 days ≈ 3.43 days)**  (d) 4 days  
- **Solution:** Combined rate = 1/24 + 1/6 + 1/12 = (1+4+2)/24 = 7/24 → Days = **24/7**

**Q17.** A and B completed work together in 5 days. Had A worked at twice speed and B at half speed, it would take 4 days. How long for A alone?  
- **(a) 15 days**  (b) 10 days  (c) 20 days  (d) 12 days  
- **Solution:** 1/A + 1/B = 1/5; 2/A + 1/(2B) = 1/4 → Solving: A = **15 days**

**Q18.** X is four times as efficient as Y. Both together complete work in 20 days. How many days for X alone?  
- **(a) 25 days**  (b) 20 days  (c) 30 days  (d) 50 days  
- **Solution:** X = 4Y → 4Y + Y = 5Y × 20 = 100 work units → X alone = 100/4Y… → **25 days**

**Q19.** 12 men can complete a work in 18 days. After 6 days, 4 more men join. How many more days to complete?  
- **(a) 9 days**  (b) 10 days  (c) 12 days  (d) 8 days  
- **Solution:** Total work = 12×18 = 216 units. Done in 6 days = 72. Remaining 144 by 16 men = 144/16 = **9 days**

**Q20.** Two pipes A and B can fill a tank in 36 hours and 45 hours. If both opened together, how long to fill?  
- **(a) 20 hours)**  (b) 24 hours  (c) 18 hours  (d) 15 hours  
- **Solution:** 1/36 + 1/45 = (5+4)/180 = 9/180 = 1/20 → **20 hours**

### 🚗 Category 5: Time, Speed and Distance

**Q21.** A car travels 540 km in 6 hours. What is its speed in m/s?  
- **(a) 25 m/s**  (b) 30 m/s  (c) 90 m/s  (d) 15 m/s  
- **Solution:** Speed = 540/6 = 90 km/hr = 90 × (5/18) = **25 m/s**

**Q22.** Two cars start from A and B traveling towards each other at 50 kmph and 60 kmph. If AB = 330 km, where do they meet from A?  
- **(a) 150 km**  (b) 180 km  (c) 165 km  (d) 200 km  
- **Solution:** Time = 330/110 = 3 hrs → Distance from A = 50×3 = **150 km**

**Q23.** A man covers half a distance at 40 kmph and the rest at 60 kmph. Find average speed.  
- **(a) 48 kmph**  (b) 50 kmph  (c) 45 kmph  (d) 52 kmph  
- **Solution:** Average = 2×40×60/(40+60) = 4800/100 = **48 kmph**

**Q24.** A train 200m long passes a pole in 10 seconds. What is the speed in km/hr?  
- **(a) 72 km/hr**  (b) 60 km/hr  (c) 80 km/hr  (d) 54 km/hr  
- **Solution:** Speed = 200/10 = 20 m/s = 20 × 18/5 = **72 km/hr**

**Q25.** A boat goes 20 km downstream in 2 hours and 10 km upstream in 2 hours. Find speed of stream.  
- **(a) 2.5 km/hr**  (b) 5 km/hr  (c) 3 km/hr  (d) 7.5 km/hr  
- **Solution:** Downstream = 10 km/hr, Upstream = 5 km/hr → Stream = (10−5)/2 = **2.5 km/hr**

### 📐 Category 6: Ratio, Proportion & Averages

**Q26.** If a:b = 5:7 and b:c = 7:9, find a:b:c.  
- **(a) 5:7:9**  (b) 5:7:8  (c) 7:5:9  (d) 5:9:7  
- **Solution:** b is common → a:b:c = **5:7:9**

**Q27.** The average of 5 numbers is 42. If one number is removed, the average becomes 40. What is the removed number?  
- **(a) 50**  (b) 48  (c) 45  (d) 52  
- **Solution:** Sum = 210, New sum = 160 → Removed = 210 − 160 = **50**

**Q28.** In a mixture of 100 liters, ratio of acid to water is 3:1. How much water must be added to make ratio 3:4?  
- **(a) 75 liters**  (b) 50 liters  (c) 100 liters  (d) 60 liters  
- **Solution:** Acid = 75L, Water = 25L. New ratio 75:(25+x) = 3:4 → 25+x = 100 → x = **75 liters**

**Q29.** Divide ₹3000 among A, B, C in ratio 2:3:5.  
- **(a) ₹600, ₹900, ₹1500**  (b) ₹500, ₹1000, ₹1500  (c) ₹700, ₹900, ₹1400  (d) ₹600, ₹1000, ₹1400  
- **Solution:** A = 3000×2/10 = 600, B = 900, C = 1500 → **₹600, ₹900, ₹1500**

**Q30.** The average age of 30 students is 15 years. If the teacher's age is included, it becomes 16 years. Find teacher's age.  
- **(a) 46 years**  (b) 40 years  (c) 45 years  (d) 50 years  
- **Solution:** Teacher = 31×16 − 30×15 = 496 − 450 = **46 years**

### 💵 Category 7: Simple & Compound Interest

**Q31.** Find the simple interest on ₹5000 at 8% per annum for 3 years.  
- **(a) ₹1200**  (b) ₹1000  (c) ₹1500  (d) ₹800  
- **Solution:** SI = 5000 × 8 × 3/100 = **₹1200**

**Q32.** The compound interest on ₹10000 at 10% per annum for 2 years is:  
- (a) ₹2000  **(b) ₹2100**  (c) ₹2200  (d) ₹1900  
- **Solution:** CI = 10000(1.1² − 1) = 10000 × 0.21 = **₹2100**

**Q33.** At what rate of SI does a sum become double in 8 years?  
- **(a) 12.5%**  (b) 10%  (c) 15%  (d) 8%  
- **Solution:** SI = P → P×R×8/100 = P → R = **12.5%**

### 🧠 Category 8: Reasoning — Series & Patterns

**Q34.** Find the next number: 2, 6, 12, 20, 30, ?  
- **(a) 42**  (b) 36  (c) 44  (d) 40  
- **Solution:** Differences: 4, 6, 8, 10, **12** → Next = 30+12 = **42** (Pattern: n(n+1))

**Q35.** Find the missing: 3, 9, 27, 81, ?  
- **(a) 243**  (b) 162  (c) 324  (d) 729  
- **Solution:** Each × 3 → 81 × 3 = **243**

**Q36.** Complete: A, D, G, J, ?  
- **(a) M**  (b) L  (c) N  (d) K  
- **Solution:** Gap of 3 letters → J + 3 = **M**

**Q37.** Find odd one out: 2, 5, 10, 17, 26, 37, 50, 64  
- (a) 37  (b) 26  **(c) 64**  (d) 50  
- **Solution:** Pattern is n²+1: 1,4,9,16,25,36,49 → 50+1=**65** not 64. Odd one = **64**

### 👨‍👩‍👧‍👦 Category 9: Reasoning — Blood Relations

**Q38.** Pointing to a photograph, Arun said "She is the daughter of my grandfather's only son." How is the girl related to Arun?  
- **(a) Sister**  (b) Cousin  (c) Daughter  (d) Mother  
- **Solution:** Grandfather's only son = Father → Father's daughter = **Sister**

**Q39.** A is father of B. C is son of D. B is married to D. How is A related to C?  
- **(a) Grandfather**  (b) Father  (c) Uncle  (d) Brother  
- **Solution:** A→Father of B, B married D, C is son of D → A is **Grandfather** of C

### 🔤 Category 10: Reasoning — Coding-Decoding

**Q40.** If COMPUTER is coded as RFUVQNPC, how is MEDICINE coded?  
- **(a) EOJDJEFM**  (b) FDJDJEFM  (c) EOJDKEFM  (d) EOJDJEFL  
- **Solution:** Each letter is coded with the reverse of the word order with shifting patterns. *(Pattern-based — practice on IndiaBix)*

### 📝 Category 11: Verbal Ability (Sample Questions)

**Q41.** Choose the correct synonym of "BENEVOLENT":  
- (a) Hostile  **(b) Generous**  (c) Cruel  (d) Selfish

**Q42.** Choose the correct antonym of "OBSCURE":  
- (a) Vague  (b) Hidden  **(c) Clear**  (d) Dark

**Q43.** Spot the error: "Neither of the two students (a) / have completed (b) / their assignments (c) / No error (d)"  
- **(b) "have" should be "has"** — "Neither" takes a singular verb.

**Q44.** Fill in the blank: "He has been working here ___ 2015."  
- (a) from  **(b) since**  (c) for  (d) by  
- **Solution:** "Since" is used for a point in time.

**Q45.** Arrange the sentence: P: "very useful" Q: "is" R: "computer" S: "a machine"  
- **(a) RQSP** → "Computer is a machine very useful" → Best logical order.

### 📊 Category 12: Data Interpretation

**Q46.** A company's sales (in lakhs) for 5 years: 2019: 50, 2020: 60, 2021: 45, 2022: 75, 2023: 80. What is the average annual sales?  
- **(a) 62 lakhs**  (b) 60 lakhs  (c) 65 lakhs  (d) 58 lakhs  
- **Solution:** (50+60+45+75+80)/5 = 310/5 = **62 lakhs**

**Q47.** From the above data, what is the percentage increase from 2021 to 2022?  
- (a) 60%  **(b) 66.67%**  (c) 50%  (d) 75%  
- **Solution:** (75−45)/45 × 100 = **66.67%**

### 🎲 Category 13: Advanced — Permutations, Combinations & Probability

**Q48.** In how many ways can 5 students be arranged in a row?  
- **(a) 120**  (b) 60  (c) 24  (d) 720  
- **Solution:** 5! = **120**

**Q49.** From a group of 7 men and 4 women, a committee of 5 is to be formed with at least 2 women. How many ways?  
- **(a) 301**  (b) 210  (c) 350  (d) 462  
- **Solution:** C(4,2)×C(7,3) + C(4,3)×C(7,2) + C(4,4)×C(7,1) = 6×35 + 4×21 + 1×7 = 210+84+7 = **301**

**Q50.** A bag contains 5 red and 3 blue balls. Two balls are drawn at random. Probability that both are red?  
- **(a) 5/14**  (b) 10/28  (c) 15/56  (d) 1/4  
- **Solution:** C(5,2)/C(8,2) = 10/28 = **5/14**

**Q51.** A 4-digit code is formed from digits 0-9 (no repetition). What's the probability that it starts with 5?  
- **(a) 1/10**  (b) 1/9  (c) 504/5040  (d) 1/8  
- **Solution:** Total codes starting with non-zero = 9×9×8×7; Those starting with 5 = 1×9×8×7 → Probability considering all = **1/9** (since first digit ≠ 0, effectively 1 out of 9 choices)

**Q52.** What is the mean of first 10 natural numbers?  
- **(a) 5.5**  (b) 5  (c) 6  (d) 10  
- **Solution:** Sum = 55, Mean = 55/10 = **5.5**

**Q53.** The marks of 5 students are: 40, 50, 60, 70, 80. Find the standard deviation.  
- **(a) √200 ≈ 14.14**  (b) 10  (c) 15  (d) 20  
- **Solution:** Mean = 60; Variance = [(20²+10²+0+10²+20²)/5] = 1000/5 = 200 → SD = **√200 ≈ 14.14**

## 7. Tips, Tricks & Shortcuts

### 🧮 Quantitative Aptitude Shortcuts

| Concept | Shortcut |
|---------|----------|
| **Percentage ↔ Fraction** | 1/2=50%, 1/3=33.3%, 1/4=25%, 1/5=20%, 1/6=16.67%, 1/8=12.5% |
| **Successive % change** | Net = a + b + ab/100 |
| **Profit % (CP₁ = SP₂)** | If CP of N₁ = SP of N₂, Profit% = [(N₁−N₂)/N₂] × 100 |
| **Average Speed** | Two equal distances at speeds a,b → Avg = 2ab/(a+b) |
| **Time & Work** | If A does in x days, B in y days → Together = xy/(x+y) |
| **Train crossing** | Same direction: (L₁+L₂)/(S₁−S₂); Opposite: (L₁+L₂)/(S₁+S₂) |
| **SI Doubling Time** | T = 100/R years |
| **CI compounding** | A = P(1+R/100)ⁿ |
| **nPr** | n!/(n−r)! |
| **nCr** | n!/[r!(n−r)!] |

### 🧠 Reasoning Shortcuts

| Concept | Tip |
|---------|-----|
| **Blood Relations** | Always draw family tree diagrams; use + for male, − for female |
| **Coding-Decoding** | Check: reverse alphabet position (A=26, B=25...), +1/−1 shift, mirror coding |
| **Syllogism** | Use Venn diagrams for ALL scenarios; check "Some" vs "All" vs "No" |
| **Seating** | Fix one person first, then place others relative to them |
| **Direction** | Draw a compass; track turns carefully |
| **Series** | Check: differences, ratios, squares, cubes, primes, alternating patterns |

### 🔤 Verbal Ability Tips

| Concept | Tip |
|---------|-----|
| **Error Spotting** | Check: subject-verb agreement, tense consistency, preposition usage, article rules |
| **RC Passages** | Read questions FIRST, then scan passage for answers; save time |
| **Synonyms/Antonyms** | Learn root words (Latin/Greek): "bene"=good, "mal"=bad, "omni"=all |
| **Sentence Improvement** | Look for: redundancy, wrong idiom usage, parallelism errors |
| **Parajumbles** | Find the opening sentence (introduction/topic), then look for logical connectors |
